New York City was thrown into delightful disarray yesterday as a skateboarding dog named Ollie wreaked havoc across the boroughs. "He's got better moves than most skaters in Brooklyn," said Tony, a bewildered pedestrian. Authorities attempted to apprehend Ollie, but he evaded capture by ollie- ing over a police cruiser and disappearing into a bodega. Giant Spiders Invade Australia; Military Stuck in Web of Chaos

In a development straight out of a horror movie, Australia has officially been overrun by an army of giant spiders, and the military is having a tough time keeping eight legs ahead. The arachnids, some reportedly the size of compact cars, have taken control of major cities, weaving webs over iconic landmarks like the Sydney Opera House and even setting up a "spider toll booth" on the Harbour Bridge. "They don't even take cash," lamented one Sydney commuter, "only flies or crickets." The spiders seem particularly drawn to BBQ areas, leading to a nationwide sausage shortage that has sent Australians into a collective panic.

Military operations, dubbed "Operation Arachnid Unwind," have so far been less than successful. Tanks are reportedly no match for the spiders' silk, which experts describe as "strong enough to stop a boomerang mid-flight." Troops have resorted to flamethrowers, but this has only resulted in what experts are calling "angry, flaming spiders," making the situation exponentially worse. In a desperate move, officials have called for volunteers to help, armed only with oversized flip-flops and cans of bug spray. "This is why we tell people not to import exotic pets," said one exhausted general as a spider casually climbed onto his Humvee. Meanwhile, scientists are scrambling to find a solution, though one researcher warned, "If these things learn to surf, it’s game over for all of us."
